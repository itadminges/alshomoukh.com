The Early Years Foundation Stage Curriculum
Al Shomoukh International Private School follows the Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) Framework which is adapted to meet the needs of each individual child. EYFS EYFS) sets standards for the learning, development and care of the child.

Primary Curriculum
We follow the Primary Key Stage 1 and 2 UK National Curriculum. The curriculum sets a clear statutory entitlement to learning for all children which is both inclusive and challenging. Our teachers plan a range of current and relevant topics carefully matched to meet the requirements of the UK National Curriculum. Emphasis is placed on developing creativity, problem solving and physical development to support the development of the whole child. To enrich students’ learning, we engage in innovative collaboration in English, Mathematics, Science, Art, Computing, Physical Education (PE), Music and Life Skills. We aim to develop our students to be independent learners who are stimulated to be curious and ask questions.

Key Stage 1 & Lower Key Stage 2
Our Key Stage 1 curriculum builds on skills and the knowledge acquired in the Early Years Foundation Stage. To support our Key Stage 1 curriculum, we have developed a systematic phonics approach that teaches students a comprehensive set of letter-sound relationships. Students in Grade 1 & 2 have a daily phonics lesson, the school also continues to provide catch-up phonics lessons to students in Grade 3.  
Lower Key Stage 2 (Grades 3 -6)
The lower Key Stage 2 curriculum offers a variety of ways for students to express their creativity. Our highly qualified teachers focus on developing students independent learning skills and creating a nurturing and supportive environment that allows students to learn more about themselves and others. From Grade 3 to Grade 6 we have specialist teachers who ensure our students get the best provision possible in PE, Art, ICT, Music and French.
Reading Scheme
At Al Shomoukh International School we follow the Oxford Reading Tree program to teach reading and to ensure our students become confident readers. The reading program introduces reading challenges in Grade 2 to enrich children’s vocabulary and reading range. Guided reading is introduced in Grade 1 and is integrated into the curriculum, students have a daily guided reading session. The Oxford Reading Tree book bands indicate the reading level for each reading book and are used across different schemes.

Omani General Education Diploma (GED) Grades 11 & 12
Oman’s General Education Diploma (GED) is available to students in Grade 11 & 12 and is equivalent to the International A-Levels program. The curriculum is developed and monitored by the Ministry of Education. The Omani GED curriculum at Al Shomoukh International School inspires students to be compelling individuals who are curious about all areas of learning. Our highly qualified specialist teachers will provide students with the skills, competencies and knowledge to be confident individuals who will continue pursuing their further education. S.I.S students have an option to study for the GED from Grade 10.
The Core Curriculum subjects for Grade 10 are Mathematics, English, Triple Science (Biology, Physics & Chemistry), ICT, Physical Education, Music and Art. Compulsory subjects for Arabic speaking learners are Arabic, Islamic and Arabic Social Studies.  
 
The Core Curriculum subjects for Grade 11 & 12 are Mathematics, English, Science (Biology, physics & Chemistry) – students have an option to choose one, two three science subjects. One science must be selected Options: ICT, Physical Education, Music, Art, Business Compulsory subjects for Arabic speaking learners are Arabic, Islamic and Arabic Social Studies

FAQ:
What is the academic level criteria that has to be met in order to admit my child to S.I.S?
At Al Shomoukh International Private School, we assess each child's academic readiness through a placement test to ensure they are well-prepared for the curriculum and will thrive in our learning environment. Our goal is to provide the necessary support for students to succeed and achieve their potential.

What is the age criteria for accepting students in KG1, KG2, and Grade 1?
•	KG1: Children must be 3 years old and 2 months by September 1st of the academic year.
•	KG2: Children must be 4 years and 2 months old by September 1st of the academic year.
•	Grade 1: Children must be 6 years and 2 months old by September 1st of the academic year.
Can I meet the teachers?
Yes, we encourage parent-teacher collaboration. You can meet with teachers during scheduled parent-teacher conferences or by making an appointment through our school administration. Our teachers are always available to discuss your child’s progress and address any questions or concerns.
When do students start learning French?
Students begin learning French from Grade 1 as part of our foreign language curriculum. This provides early exposure to the language and fosters bilingual communication skills, which are valuable in today’s globalized world.
Do you organize trips outside Oman?
Yes, we believe that learning extends beyond the classroom. We organize educational trips outside of Oman for older students to enhance their learning experiences. These trips are carefully planned to complement the curriculum and provide cultural, historical, and practical learning opportunities. We also arrange local trips within Oman to support student learning in various subjects.
